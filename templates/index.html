Fusarium Wilt (caused by the soilborne fungus Fusarium oxysporum) is a widespread and economically important disease.

Prevention and control

  • Once Fusarium Wilt is infected, there is no effective treatment.
  • Remove and dispose effected plant immediately.
  • Remove and replace fusarium-infected garden soil.
  • Fungus populations can be reduced by soil solarization and fumigation.
  • The use of resistant cultivars and pathogen-free cotton seed are good preventions of disease.

Cotton leaf curl disease (CLCuD) is a viral infection of cotton and other susceptible host plants that is transmitted by silverleaf whitefly (Bemisia tabaci).

Prevention and control

  • Immediately remove infected-looking plants and bury them.
  • Protect seedlings from whiteflies and control weed.
  • Use resistant or tolerant cultivars.
